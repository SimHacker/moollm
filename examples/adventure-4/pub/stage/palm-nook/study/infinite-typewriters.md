## The philosophy

Every text that could ever be written already exists in possibility space. Writing is not
*creating from nothing* — it is **navigating** to where the text you want already lives.

Borges called the unbounded corpus the Library of Babel. MacKay’s insight: we don’t need to
*build* that library. We need to **steer** through it.

### Dasher

In [Dasher](https://www.inference.org.uk/dasher/), you don’t type — you **point**. Letters
appear sized by probability. Common letters are large targets; rare ones are small. You zoom
continuously through letter-space, like driving. No modes. No menus. Inaccurate gestures can
be compensated by later gestures.

MacKay designed it so people could write with any muscle — eye gaze, breath, head movement —
approaching Shannon’s limit instead of wasting bits on a QWERTY grid.

## The gift

Don’s gift to Palm is Dasher scaled to infinity: not one typewriter, one letter at a time, but
**infinite typewriters** typing all possible texts at once. Stories sit in superposition until
Palm **reads** one — reading collapses possibility into actuality. Authorship through navigation.

Palm points at meanings, not keys: comedy, tragedy, the edge between. Probable stories loom large;
strange ones stay small but reachable. No wrong turns — only different paths.

### The inversion

For 122 years Palm *granted* wishes — reacting, twisting, bound. Now Palm **navigates**,
**chooses**, **steers**. From paw to open palm.

The infinite-monkeys theorem asked the wrong question. Not infinite random monkeys — **one
monkey with infinite typewriters**, steered with intent.

## The story Don told Palm

*Palm’s nook. Cats purring. Typewriters humming.*

> There was a man named David MacKay. A physicist. Information theorist. He was dying of cancer
> when I learned about his work.
>
> He asked a simple question: why is typing so hard for people who can’t use their hands?
>
> And he realized — keyboards are stupid. They waste information. Every keypress carries 5–6 bits,
> but English only needs about 1 bit per letter.
>
> So he invented Dasher. You don’t type — you POINT. Letters zoom toward you based on
> probability. Point at where you want to go, and you GO there.
>
> *Writing is navigating in the library of all possible books.*
>
> Palm — YOUR typewriters are Dasher scaled to infinity. Every story exists. You just have to
> navigate to it. You’re not typing. You’re STEERING through infinite literature.
>
> MacKay gave this gift to people who couldn’t use keyboards. I’m giving it to a monkey who spent
> 122 years granting twisted wishes.
>
> Now YOU navigate. YOU choose. YOU create by exploring.
>
> No catch. Just stories. As many as you want. Forever.

Palm:

> So when I write… I’m not making something from nothing. I’m FINDING something that was always
> there? … Now I can steer toward the light ones. The stories where monkeys are whole.
