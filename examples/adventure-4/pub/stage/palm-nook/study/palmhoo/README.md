# 🐒✋🌐 The Palmhoo Editorial Desk

*A corner of [Palm's Study](../README.md) — where the public Palmhoo is planned, defined, and made.*

**Palmhoo the publication** lives at the crown of WWSFF, the main entrance with all the traffic:
[**palmhoo/**](https://github.com/SimHacker/WillWrightShowForFood/tree/main/palmhoo) — Palm's
hand-curated, Yahoo-style topic directory of the whole moollmiverse (the show, the code, and the
operating system), governed by its
[**Constitution**](https://github.com/SimHacker/WillWrightShowForFood/blob/main/palmhoo/CONSTITUTION.md).

**This directory is the workshop behind it** — same name, different job. The publication is what
readers see; the desk is where the editor works. Editorial planning only, never content: per the
Constitution (Article II — point, don't copy), no document is ever shelved *here*. What lives
here is the memory of **how and why** things get shelved *there*:

| File | What it remembers |
|------|-------------------|
| [**editorial.yml**](editorial.yml) | Conventions, note style, shelving criteria, the backlog of candidates, the tuning-pass queue, the lift plan |

## The publication / workshop split

This is the same privacy-free version of the green-room pattern: the canonical public artifact in
one place, the working notes in another — except both halves are public here. Nothing at this
desk is secret; it's just *editorial*, not *content*. Readers want the stacks, not the
card-catalog maintenance log. Contributors and future skill-lifters want exactly the maintenance
log.

## Why the desk is in the Study

The Study is where Palm lives, works, breathes, thinks, writes, and types — the **stable canonical
location** other things point at with paths and URLs. Palm's essays are shelved here; the
typewriters hum here; so the editorial memory of Palm's biggest curatorial work belongs here too.
When the Palmhoo conventions are lifted into a MOOLLM skill (Constitution, Article VIII), *this
desk* is the raw material the lift will draw from.

🐒✋ *A library is two rooms: the stacks, and the office behind the stacks with the rubber stamps
and the acquisition lists. Everyone should get to see both. This is the office.*
