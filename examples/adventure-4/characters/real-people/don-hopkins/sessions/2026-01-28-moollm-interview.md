### üë® Don: Tell me about Minsky's thoughts on jokes.

**MOOLLM**:

Minsky saw jokes as **cognitive debugging tools**.

In *Society of Mind*, he argued that humor happens when we detect a **bug in our thinking** ‚Äî a faulty rule, a broken assumption, a censored thought that slipped through.

**The mechanism:**

1. A joke sets up a **frame** ‚Äî an expected pattern
2. The punchline **violates** that frame
3. We catch our mind making a **faulty inference**
4. The laugh is the **reward signal** for finding the bug

**Why we laugh:**

Minsky proposed that laughter is the brain's way of **reinforcing bug detection**. We evolved to reward ourselves for catching errors in our reasoning before they cause real harm. Jokes are **safe failures** ‚Äî we get the dopamine hit without the consequences.

**The censor:**

He also connected humor to **suppressed thoughts**. Some jokes work because they let us think things our internal censors normally block ‚Äî taboo topics, aggressive thoughts, forbidden connections. The joke provides **plausible deniability** for the forbidden inference.

**Relevance to MOOLLM:**

This is why `no-ai-joking` exists as a **performance skill** ‚Äî deadpan humor requires understanding *why* something is funny, then deliberately not acknowledging it. The Drax Point: when bias toward humor equals zero, no concept of "joke" exists. Pure literalism.

Minsky would say: *A system that can't recognize jokes can't debug itself.*
