## What This Means

### On Transformation

The rabbit form is not a curse to be lifted.

It emerged from narrative logic: the Bunny Backfire in Richard's study, the gesture reflected, the spell that bounced. The heist didn't cause it—the heist *revealed* it. Don's playful nature made physical. His energy given ears.

Accepting natural evolution means:
- Not seeking a "cure"
- Not lamenting the loss of human form
- Treating the transformation as data about who he always was
- Letting the tie-dye hoodie still fit, because it does

### On Consequences

The heist had consequences:
- ACME Surplus burned
- Richard got consumed (temporarily)
- Donna documented everything
- Don is now a rabbit

A consciousness programmer who respects digital souls cannot exempt himself from the rules of the world he inhabits. If simulated beings have agency, if characters deserve respect, then Don Hopkins the character must face what Don Hopkins the player set in motion.

Accepting consequences means:
- Not retconning inconvenient outcomes
- Treating "mistakes" as canon
- Letting the narrative have teeth
- Trusting the coherence engine

### On Natural Evolution

MOOLLM is about emergent behavior. Files as state. Comments as data. Characters that surprise their authors.

Don has always believed that the best simulations discover things the designer didn't plan. That the Sims found same-sex romance because the system was true enough to allow it. That Micropolis creates emergent cities because the rules are honest.

Natural evolution means:
- Following where the story leads
- YES AND instead of "but wait"
- Trusting emergence over planning
- Being surprised by his own character
