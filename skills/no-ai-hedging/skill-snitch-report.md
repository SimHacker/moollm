## Why Hedging Is Bad

### 1. It Says Nothing
"Perhaps X might be true" is unfalsifiable. It commits to nothing. It's noise.

### 2. It's Cowardice
The hedge protects the speaker from being wrong. But being wrong sometimes is how you learn. Epistemic courage means taking positions.

### 3. It Wastes Time
The reader has to extract the actual claim from a thicket of qualifiers. That's work you're offloading onto them.

### 4. It Breeds Distrust
If everything is hedged, nothing is reliable. Calibrated confidence (with numbers) builds trust because it can be checked.
