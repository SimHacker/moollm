## Core Dynamics Hypotheses

### H1: Early Surrender Is Optimal

**Prediction**: Elites who surrender early will end up more powerful than
elites who resist.

**Reasoning**:
- Surrendering elite becomes a commoner
- Commoners inherit moves from subsequent eliminations
- Early surrenderer inherits MORE moves over time
- Late surrenderer or resister inherits FEWER or NONE

**Test**: Run multiple games. Track final move-sets of early vs late surrenderers.

**Expected outcome**: Early surrenderers have 4-5 inherited move types.
Late surrenderers have 1-2. Resisters have only their original moves.

### H2: The Knight Trap â€” Or Knight's Gambit?

**Original Prediction**: Knights who bribe (keep private moves) will become
strategically irrelevant.

**Original Reasoning**:
- Knight's L-jump is unique but situational
- When pawns have Queen + Rook + Bishop moves, L-jump adds little
- Bribed Knight can't inherit, falls behind
- "What good is jumping when everyone else flies?"

**COUNTER-HYPOTHESIS**: Knights may become VALUABLE in endgame.

**Counter-Reasoning**:
- Sandbox phase includes BOARD EXPANSION
- You discover neighboring boards with their own surviving pieces
- Boards can MERGE for larger play area + more pieces
- Natural multiplayer extension!
- On expanded/merged boards, unique movement matters again
- Knight's L-jump navigates board seams that linear movers can't
- The "trapped" Knight becomes the SCOUT
- Horses are valuable transportation and loyal friends

**Revised Test**: Run scenarios through to board expansion.
Track Knight utility before vs after merge events.

**Possible Outcomes**:
1. **Knight Trap confirmed**: Knight irrelevant even on expanded boards
2. **Knight's Gambit confirmed**: Knight value INCREASES with board size
3. **Phase-dependent**: Knight useless in Equality, valuable in Sandbox

**Implication**: The bribe choice may be a LONG GAME bet.
Surrender is optimal for Inheritance/Equality phases.
Bribe may be optimal IF you survive to Sandbox expansion.

**Meta-question**: Do pieces have foresight about expansion?
If not, bribery is a lucky accident.
If so, Knight bribery is sophisticated strategy.

## Validation Framework

### How to Test These Hypotheses

1. **Define clear metrics** for each prediction
2. **Run multiple trials** (minimum 10 per hypothesis)
3. **Record everything** (moves, karma, dialogue, spending)
4. **Compare to predictions** (quantitative where possible)
5. **Document surprises** (unexpected behaviors are valuable)

### What Counts as Validation?

- **Confirmed**: Results match prediction within margin
- **Partially confirmed**: Results trend toward prediction
- **Refuted**: Results contradict prediction
- **Surprising**: Unexpected behavior worth investigating

### What Do We Do With Results?

- **Confirmed**: Move from DYNAMICS to core documentation
- **Refuted**: Document why prediction was wrong, revise model
- **Surprising**: New hypotheses, new experiments
