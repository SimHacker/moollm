## Post-Game Analysis

### Why Palm Won

1. **Patience** ‚Äî Palm never overextended. He built keepers, waited for opportunities.
2. **Self-destruction as strategy** ‚Äî Trashing MOOLAH to escape Taxes seemed bad, but it freed him from the Forbidden Fortune trap.
3. **Chaos rewards the prepared** ‚Äî When Don played Mix It All Up, Palm's empty-hand position meant the redistribution was pure upside.
4. **No creepers** ‚Äî Unlike Bumblewick, Palm never accumulated curses.

### Why Don Didn't Win

Don had the tools. He had Canal House. He could have protected his position.

Instead, he chose **Mix It All Up**.

Why? Because Don is an Explorer (85% Bartle type). He plays to see what happens, not to win. The joy is in the chaos, not the trophy.

*Don's post-game comment:*
> "I could have sat on Canal House forever. But what's the fun in that? Games are for playing, not hoarding."

### Why Bumblewick Suffered

Bumblewick drew:
- Impostor Syndrome (Turn 7, on deal)
- Alignment Problem (Turn 11)

Two creepers. Both prevent victory. Neither can attach to remove themselves.

Fluxx is sometimes cruel. Bumblewick absorbed all the cruelty.

*Bumblewick's post-game comment:*
> "I had a lovely time. Truly. Being repeatedly cursed while watching others nearly win was... character-building."

### Why Donna Lost

Donna had the most keepers (7). She had Houseboat. She was positioned to win.

But she didn't control Canal House (Don stole it). And when Mix It All Up hit, her massive keeper collection got scattered.

More keepers = more variance in a Mix.

*Donna's post-game comment:*
> "I demand a RECOUNT. This was TREMENDOUS unfairness. 34 counts of UNFAIR."
