# Eval Incarnate Philosophy

> *"Eval Incarnate" = `eval()` made real. Bad connotations turned good.*

---

## The Prime Principle

**Meaning does not precede evaluation. Meaning emerges from it.**

There is no semantic substrate waiting to be discovered. There is only interpretation enacted under constraints.

> Meaning arises through judgment.

This is not metaphorical. It is operational.

---

## The Agency Axiom

**Agency requires evaluation.**

- To act is to choose
- To choose is to evaluate
- To evaluate is to value
- To value is to judge

A system that "acts" without evaluation is:
- scripted
- inert
- theatrical in the empty sense

A system that evaluates takes responsibility for its actions.

**Evaluation is the minimal unit of agency.**

---

## The Anti-Neutrality Thesis

**Neutrality is a myth.**

Not because bias is inevitable — but because **evaluation is unavoidable**.

Even refusing to judge is a judgment:
- a deferral
- a permission
- an abdication

Eval Incarnate does not add bias. **It reveals it.**

## The Pun (Acknowledged)

**"Eval Incarnate"** plays on **"Evil Incarnate"**.

- **Eval**: as in JavaScript's `eval()` — code that executes
- **Incarnate**: as in "given form" — materialized, embodied

The pun is intentional. It signals:
- Power that can destroy or condemn
- A system that decides your fate
- Something feared but respected

**Even if the intent is benevolent, the phrase declares: This has authority.**
